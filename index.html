The GRC Engineering Cheat Sheet

For decades, auditors and governments defined and molded Legacy GRC in their image. Today, engineers and analysts are transforming it into something new: GRC Engineering. This cheat sheet outlines what makes GRC Engineering different.

GRC

“Governance, risk, and compliance (GRC) are three related facets that aim to assure an organization reliably achieves objectives, addresses uncertainty and acts with integrity.”
— Wikipedia

+
Engineering

“Engineering is the practice of using natural science, mathematics, and the engineering design process to solve problems within technology, increase efficiency and productivity, and improve systems.”
— Wikipedia

=
GRC Engineering

GRC Engineering is the practice of using science, math, user-centered design, and modern software development to assure an organization reliably achieves objectives, addresses uncertainty, and acts with integrity, all while continuously improving its efficiency, productivity, and systems.

Legacy GRC vs. GRC Engineering at a glance

Program Legacy GRC GRC Engineering
All
  • Framework-first focus
  • Documentation-heavy work products
  • Outputs conflated with outcomes
  • GRC treated as internal programs that serve the GRC team’s needs
  • Realistic risk-centered focus
  • Threat-informed everything: policies, controls, trainings, etc.
  • Systems thinking applied across the board: organizational governance, risk analysis, control modeling, etc.
  • Design thinking harnessed to make the right thing to do the easy thing to do
  • GRC treated as a product that serves internal and external customers’ needs

  • Policies, standards, procedures
  • Docs =/= control reality
  • Metric-less committees & decisions
  • Annual/semi-annual training (boring)
  • PaC enforces “risk tolerance” (pre-deploy/change)
  • “Autocorrect/reconcile” docs ↔ controls
  • Metrics-focused committees & decisions
  • Real-time behavioral interventions & scientific pedagogy
  • Policy-[as|to|from]-Code

  • Qualitative risk analysis (manual)
  • Subjective data & heatmaps
  • Fragmented weaknesses & issues
  • Accountability police
  • Fear, Uncertainty, & Doubt (FUD)

  • Quantitative risk analysis (automated)
  • Objective data & histograms
  • Holistic risk scenarios (threat + vector + asset + impact)
  • Decision support partners
  • Evidence, Logic, Math, Reason (ELMR >>> FUD)
  • TPRM, balanced third + first-party focus

  • Periodic, isolated control monitoring
  • Evidence samples
  • Automated, holistic control monitoring & active testing
  • Evidence populations (full)

Terms

Term Description
Systems ThinkingExamining how components interrelate and work together over time within larger systems. Applied across governance, risk analysis, and control modeling.
Design ThinkingHuman-centered problem-solving methodology. Harnessed to make the right thing to do the easy thing to do.
Threat-InformedGrounding policies, controls, and trainings in real-world threat intelligence rather than abstract framework checklists.
GRC as a ProductTreating GRC programs as products serving internal and external customers, with user research, feedback loops, and measurable outcomes.
Policy-as-Code (PaC)Policies written as executable code; the code is the source of truth, enabling version control, testing, and deterministic enforcement.
Policy-to-CodeTranslating human-readable policy documents into executable code, bridging policy authors and enforcement systems.
Policy-from-CodeDeriving policy documentation from code, configurations, or runtime behavior. Closes the gap between docs and control reality.
Scientific PedagogyEvidence-based learning science—spaced repetition, scenario-based exercises, measurable retention—applied to security training.
TPCMThird-party compliance management. Legacy questionnaire-focused approach that conflates compliance with risk.
TPRMThird-party risk management. Balanced third + first-party focus, evaluating real-world threat scenarios and value-at-risk.
Qualitative Risk AnalysisSubjective High/Medium/Low scales based on expert judgment. Manual, inconsistent, and difficult to aggregate.
Quantitative Risk AnalysisNumerical models, probability distributions, and measurable data. Automated, reproducible, and comparable across scenarios.
HeatmapsLegacy likelihood × impact matrices on ordinal scales. Obscure actual risk magnitude behind coarse, subjective categories.
HistogramsFrequency-distribution charts conveying risk shape, range, and confidence intervals in objective, data-driven terms.
Risk ScenariosHolistic descriptions combining threat + attack vector + affected asset + impact into a single analyzable unit.
FUDFear, Uncertainty, and Doubt. Legacy fear-based risk communication used to justify budget without rigorous analysis.
ELMREvidence, Logic, Math, Reason. The GRC Engineering alternative to FUD—grounded in verifiable data and sound reasoning.
Decision SupportProviding data, analysis, and options so stakeholders make informed risk decisions. Replaces the “accountability police” model.
Control MonitoringObserving whether controls operate as intended. GRC Engineering automates this continuously and holistically.
Active TestingExercising controls to confirm they function—not just checking they exist. Analogous to software automated tests.
Evidence SamplesLegacy subset of records selected to demonstrate control operation. Incomplete and vulnerable to selection bias.
Evidence PopulationsComplete control records collected automatically over a period. Eliminates sampling risk with full coverage.

Tools

Tool Description
OPA / RegoGeneral-purpose policy engine using the Rego declarative language. Enables Policy-as-Code in CI/CD pipelines.
Kyverno / KubewardenKubernetes-native policy engines that validate, mutate, and generate resource configurations at admission time.
BackstageOpen-source internal developer portal. Reconciles docs ↔ controls via living, system-coupled documentation.
FAIR / OpenFAIROpen standard decomposing risk into measurable factors (threat event frequency, vulnerability, loss magnitude).
MITRE ATT&CKOpen knowledge base of real-world adversary tactics, techniques, and procedures for threat-informed risk scenarios.
Monte Carlo Simulation ToolsProbabilistic simulations producing distributions and histograms instead of single-point estimates and heatmaps.
SteampipeCloud APIs as SQL tables. Full-state infrastructure queries for evidence populations across 100+ services.
CloudQueryInfrastructure-as-data platform syncing cloud and SaaS configurations into queryable databases for evidence pipelines.
ScoutSuite / ProwlerCloud security posture assessment tools. Active testing against CIS, PCI DSS, and HIPAA benchmarks.

TTPs

TTP Description
GitOps for PolicyVersion-controlled policy lifecycle with PR reviews, audit trails, and automated deployment. Replaces shared drives.
Pre-deploy Policy GatesCI/CD checks that block deployments violating governance policies (missing encryption, unapproved regions, etc.).
Nudge EngineeringReal-time behavioral interventions—context-aware prompts, guardrails, and defaults making the right action easy.
Spaced Repetition TrainingScenario-based exercises at increasing intervals. Proven retention over annual checkbox courses.
Scenario-based Risk QuantificationHolistic risk scenarios (threat + vector + asset + impact) fed through Monte Carlo simulations for loss distributions.
Risk-as-Data PipelinesETL from threat intel, vulnerability scanners, and asset inventory into unified risk models for continuous quantification.
First-party Hardening over Third-party QuestionnairesConfiguring your own blast-radius controls rather than relying on vendor compliance attestations.
Decision Briefings over Risk RegistersData-driven options and trade-off analysis for stakeholders, replacing static registers that don’t drive action.
Evidence-as-CodeProgrammatic evidence collection via APIs and infrastructure queries. Versioned, reproducible, full-population.
Continuous Control Regression TestingActive tests exercising controls on an ongoing basis—the compliance equivalent of software regression tests.
Full-population AuditQuerying 100% of control evidence records rather than sampling. Eliminates sampling bias entirely.
Trust Center as ProductTreating your trust page as a product with user research, analytics, and iterative improvement.
Auto-populated QuestionnairesMapping live control data to common schemas (SIG, CAIQ, VSA) automatically, replacing manual re-answering.
Real-time Control DashboardsLive, historically queryable views of control effectiveness replacing annual SOC 2 PDFs.
