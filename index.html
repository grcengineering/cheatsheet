The GRC Engineering Cheat Sheet

For decades, auditors and governments defined and molded Legacy GRC in their image. Today, engineers and analysts are transforming it into something new: GRC Engineering. This cheat sheet outlines what makes GRC Engineering different.

GRC

Governance, risk, and compliance (GRC) are three related facets that aim to assure an organization reliably achieves objectives, addresses uncertainty and acts with integrity. Wikipedia

Engineering

Engineering is the practice of using natural science, mathematics, and the engineering design process to solve problems within technology, increase efficiency and productivity, and improve systems. Wikipedia

Terms

Program GRC Engineering Terms
General
  • Systems Thinking — examining how components interrelate and work together over time within larger systems
  • Design Thinking — human-centered methodology to make the right thing to do the easy thing to do
  • Threat-Informed — grounding policies, controls, and trainings in real-world threat intelligence
  • GRC as a Product — treating GRC programs as products serving internal and external customers
Governance
  • Policy-as-Code — policies written as executable code; the code is the source of truth
  • Policy-to-Code — translating human-readable policies into executable code
  • Policy-from-Code — deriving policy documentation from code, configs, or runtime behavior
  • PaC — Policy as Code; enforcing governance policies via automated evaluation
  • Risk Tolerance — acceptable risk level, codified and enforced at pre-deploy/pre-change gates
  • Real-time Behavioral Interventions — context-aware nudges at the moment of risky action
  • Scientific Pedagogy — evidence-based learning science applied to security training
  • Metrics-focused Committees — governance bodies deciding on quantitative data, not subjective discussion
Risk
  • TPCM — third-party compliance management; legacy questionnaire-focused approach
  • TPRM — third-party risk management; balanced third + first-party focus
  • Qualitative Risk Analysis — subjective High/Medium/Low scales; manual and inconsistent
  • Quantitative Risk Analysis — numerical models and probability distributions; automated and reproducible
  • Heatmaps — legacy likelihood × impact matrices that obscure actual risk magnitude
  • Histograms — frequency-distribution charts conveying risk shape, range, and confidence intervals
  • Risk Scenarios — holistic threat + vector + asset + impact descriptions
  • FUD — Fear, Uncertainty, and Doubt; legacy fear-based risk communication
  • ELMR — Evidence, Logic, Math, Reason; the GRC Engineering alternative to FUD
  • Decision Support — providing data and options so stakeholders make informed risk decisions
Compliance
  • Control Monitoring — observing whether controls operate as intended; automated and continuous
  • Active Testing — exercising controls to confirm they function, not just checking they exist
  • Evidence Samples — legacy subset of records; incomplete and vulnerable to selection bias
  • Evidence Populations — complete control records collected automatically; full coverage
Trust &
Assurance
  • RFIs — Requests for Information; legacy GRC handles via email
  • Self-service RFIs — customers answer security questions through portals or automated engines
  • Real-time Visibility — transparent, up-to-date, historically browsable control status

Tools

Program Open-Source Tools
Governance
  • OPA / Rego — general-purpose policy engine; Policy-as-Code via declarative Rego language
  • Kyverno / Kubewarden — Kubernetes-native policy engines; block non-compliant workloads at admission
  • Backstage — internal developer portal; reconciles docs ↔ controls via living documentation
Risk
  • FAIR / OpenFAIR — open standard decomposing risk into measurable factors for quantitative analysis
  • MITRE ATT&CK — open knowledge base of real-world adversary tactics, techniques, and procedures
  • Monte Carlo Simulation Tools — probabilistic simulations producing distributions instead of point estimates
Compliance
  • Steampipe — cloud APIs as SQL tables; full-state infrastructure queries for evidence populations
  • CloudQuery — infrastructure-as-data platform; automated evidence pipelines and full-population audits
  • ScoutSuite / Prowler — cloud security posture tools; active testing against CIS, PCI DSS, HIPAA benchmarks

TTPs

Program Tactics, Techniques & Procedures
Governance
  • GitOps for Policy — version-controlled policy lifecycle with PR reviews, audit trails, and automated deployment
  • Pre-deploy Policy Gates — CI/CD checks that block deployments violating governance policies
  • Nudge Engineering — real-time behavioral interventions making the right action the easy action
  • Spaced Repetition Training — scenario-based exercises at increasing intervals; proven retention over annual courses
Risk
  • Scenario-based Risk Quantification — holistic risk scenarios fed through Monte Carlo simulations for loss distributions
  • Risk-as-Data Pipelines — ETL from threat intel, vuln scanners, and asset inventory into unified risk models
  • First-party Hardening over Third-party Questionnaires — blast-radius controls over vendor attestations
  • Decision Briefings over Risk Registers — data-driven options and trade-offs instead of static spreadsheets
Compliance
  • Evidence-as-Code — programmatic evidence collection via APIs; versioned, reproducible, full-population
  • Continuous Control Regression Testing — active tests exercising controls on an ongoing basis
  • Full-population Audit — querying 100% of records; eliminates sampling bias entirely
Trust &
Assurance
  • Trust Center as Product — trust page with user research, analytics, and iterative improvement
  • Auto-populated Questionnaires — mapping live control data to SIG, CAIQ, VSA schemas automatically
  • Real-time Control Dashboards — live, historically queryable views replacing annual SOC 2 PDFs
